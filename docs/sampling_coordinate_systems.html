Interception Sampling Coordinate Systems

This document pins down the distinction between world LOS sampling and camera-basis sampling for generated drone interception initial conditions. Both visualizations are fully interactive 3D — drag the colored handles to move objects, rotate the view by dragging in empty space, scroll to zoom.

Target Pursuer (camera) LOS (pursuer→target) Camera frame (+x optical, +y horiz, +z vert) Image-plane bearing (u, v) World frame

Known Objects

  • T_w: target position in world coordinates.
  • P_w: pursuer/camera position in world.
  • r: range from pursuer to target.
  • los_w: unit vector pursuer→target in world.
  • R_wc: camera-to-world rotation matrix.
  • d_c(u, v): target ray in camera coords from image bearing.

Camera Convention

  • +x_c: optical axis, forward through image center.
  • +y_c: horizontal image displacement.
  • +z_c: vertical image displacement.
  • camera_u_fraction, camera_v_fraction pick an image ray inside the FOV.

1. World position is underspecified by camera bearing alone

If we only know target position, range, and image bearing (u, v), the world position is not unique. The same image bearing can be produced from many world positions by rotating the whole camera-target geometry around the target. Below, every blue pursuer sees the target at the same image bearing.

Current generator parameterization

sample: los_azimuth_deg, los_elevation_deg camera_u_fraction, camera_v_fraction range_m derive: los_w = spherical(los_azimuth, los_elevation) P_w = T_w - range_m * los_w d_c = normalize([1, u*tan(hfov/2), v*tan(vfov/2)]) R_wc s.t. R_wc * d_c = los_w

Guarantees the target is in the FOV whenever (u, v) is inside camera limits: the camera is rotated to make the chosen image ray point at the target.

Camera-basis parameterization

sample: R_wc (camera orientation in world) camera_u_fraction, camera_v_fraction range_m derive: d_c = normalize([1, u*tan(hfov/2), v*tan(vfov/2)]) los_w = R_wc * d_c P_w = T_w - range_m * los_w

Covers the same set of world LOS directions, but the sampled primitive is camera orientation rather than pursuer position around the target.

3. Key takeaways

Confirmed: camera image bearing (u, v) plus range does not uniquely determine a world position. You must also provide either a world LOS direction or the camera basis/orientation in world.
  • Current generator samples LOS angles, then solves camera attitude.
  • Camera-basis variant samples camera orientation in world, then derives los_w and P_w.
  • Both cover the same reachable world LOS directions when their respective sampling domains line up.
  • With fixed camera roll, two angles define camera pointing; (u, v) contributes two more inside the FOV.
