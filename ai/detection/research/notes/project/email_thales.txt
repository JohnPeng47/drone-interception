Subject:  Question on observation modality in your competitive RL interception work

Dear Mr. Gavin, Dr. Lacroix, and Dr. Bronz,

I have been reading your AMIAD 2025 paper, "Agile Interception of a
Flying Target using Competitive Reinforcement Learning"
(hal-05554100), and have a question I hope you might be willing to
discuss.

The competitive PPO formulation with co-evolved pursuer and evader at
low-level control, the JAX simulator, and the indoor flight
demonstration are all very compelling. The limitations section
explicitly notes that perception was assumed perfect (mocap-based
opponent state) and that "robust perception of the target in
high-velocity flights and state estimation are required but were not
addressed." That is precisely the gap I am trying to think through,
and I would value your perspective on two points.

The first is whether you have looked at the line of work coming out of
Beihang and NWPU on monocular bearing-only interception -- in
particular, Yang et al., "High-Speed Interception Multicopter Control
by Image-based Visual Servoing" (arXiv:2404.08296, IEEE TCST 2025), and
Yan et al., "Precise Interception Flight Targets by Image-based Visual
Servoing of Multicopter" (arXiv:2409.17497, IEEE TIE 2025). They use
an analytical IBVS+PNG controller with a delayed Kalman filter,
running on the target's image-plane (u, v) coordinates plus IMU; their
observability arguments suggest bearing alone carries sufficient
information for interception, and they demonstrate real-world flights
with reasonable success. Your work and theirs feel complementary --
their controller side is much less expressive than yours, and your
perception side is fully assumed away by mocap.

The second is whether you have considered formulating the pursuer's
opponent observation in the image plane -- e.g., bbox-style (cx, cy,
w, h) plus history or a recurrent policy -- rather than as
ground-truth 3D relative position and velocity. The appeal would be
that the pursuer would not need a 3D state estimator at deployment;
the perception system would only need to provide the same kind of 2D
detection that Yang/Yan rely on, and the policy could be trained
against bbox-noise distributions measured from a real CNN detector.
Concretely, I have been wondering:

1.  Did you consider 2D image-plane observations during early
    formulation, and if so, why did you settle on 3D relative state?
2.  Do you see obstacles (sample efficiency, partial observability,
    representation capacity) that would make the 2D formulation
    significantly harder to train than the 3D one in your JAX setup?
3.  Are there ongoing or planned follow-ups in your group along the
    perception-in-the-loop direction, ideally using a learned detector
    rather than mocap?

I am drafting a research proposal that combines a synthetic-data-
trained drone detector (in the spirit of Sim2Air and SynDroneVision)
with a competitive RL controller in your style, and I would prefer
not to misrepresent the state of the art or duplicate work already in
your pipeline. Any pointers would be very much appreciated, and I am
happy to share what I learn.

Thank you for your time, and congratulations on the very nice results.
